Life cycle of Marchantia


  1. Marchantia is an expample of Bryophyta which is common for Alternation of generation (Presence of two phases Gametophyte (N) & Sporophtye(2N))

  2. Gametophyte phase (the haploid phase) starts with spores (N) which divide to give a multicellular undifferentiated structures (Protonema), but they are still simple, then go under further differenciation to be more complex.

  3. Protonema after differciating for some time, it may gives male gametophyte(bears antheridiophores) or female gametophyte(bears archegoniophores) which help later in sexual reproduction behind the sexual ones.

  4. The archegoniophore later gives archegonia which have the egg cells, and the antheridiophore also gives antherida which is full of sperms.

  5. Once the conditions are good (wet), the motile sperms keep swimming until reach the egg cell and the fertilization starts.

  6. Once the fertilization is done, the zygote (undifferentiated tissue but of course more comlex when compared with Protonema) is formed (2N), and Gametophyte phase ends and Sporophtye phase(Diploid phase) starts.

  7. The zygote keep dividing and eventually gives the Embryo

  8. The embryo keep growing and gives sporopyte (which is composed of foot, seta and capsule).

  9. once the environment is wet, The parenchematous cells(seta cells) and elators(capsule special cells) elongate and eventually the capsule rupture and the spores(sporocyte) spread and go under meiosis divison to give tetrad which is separated later and give the spores and the life cycle happens again in the same way.
